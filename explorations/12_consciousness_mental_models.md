# Consciousness Science Mental Models for Knowledge Transfer

Alternative framings of knowledge distillation (KD) through the lens of consciousness science: Integrated Information Theory (IIT), Global Workspace Theory (GWT), Higher Order Theories (HOT), Predictive Processing (PP), Attention Schema Theory (AST), Recurrent Processing Theory (RPT), and the binding problem. Consciousness science is the only field whose central question — *what makes a representation count as a representation of something to a system* — directly mirrors the failure mode of classical KD, where a student perfectly imitates a teacher's outputs without acquiring the teacher's structure. We mine that parallel for design axes.

This is the most speculative portfolio in the series. Treat it as scaffolding for hypotheses, not as physics.

---

## TL;DR

Five consciousness lenses, each reframing a different latent assumption in classical KD:

1. **Integrated Information Theory (IIT, φ)** — KD transfers *reducible mush* (per-token soft labels are a sum of independent constraints) when it should transfer *integrated structure* (constraints whose joint information exceeds the sum of their parts). The student inherits a shadow that has no φ.
2. **Global Workspace Theory** — The teacher's intelligence lives partly in *what it broadcasts vs what it suppresses*. Standard KD copies broadcast content but not the access policy that gates broadcasting. Student gets the slides, not the projector schedule.
3. **Higher Order Theories** — A teacher's representation is "about" something only because the teacher has a higher-order representation *of that representation*. KD on first-order activations transfers content; KD on the teacher's metacognitive reads (which features it is currently attending to and labeling as reliable) transfers the *aboutness* itself.
4. **Attention Schema Theory** — The teacher carries a simplified self-model of its own attention. Distilling that schema (a tiny, abstract model of what the teacher is paying attention to and why) is far cheaper than distilling raw attention maps and may be the missing inductive bias for byte-level students with no tokenizer-level structure.
5. **Recurrent Processing Theory (Lamme)** — Feedforward sweeps yield categorization; recurrence yields phenomenal binding. A purely feedforward student of a recurrent teacher loses the binding pass even when its predictions match. Recurrent depth is not architectural taste; it is a *distillation channel* the field has been blind to.

**Most absent from ML-KD:** the IIT view that *information must be integrated to be transferred as understanding*. Soft labels are sum-decomposable across positions and tokens; the teacher's actual competence is the part that *resists* this decomposition. Every other consciousness theory is a different angle on this same gap.

## 1. Integrated Information Theory (IIT) — KD as φ-Transfer

**Mechanism (one sentence).** IIT (Tononi, Oizumi, Albantakis) defines consciousness as Φ, the quantity of information generated by a system *above and beyond* the information generated by its parts taken independently — a system is conscious to the degree that its cause-effect structure is irreducible to the union of its sub-structures.

**ML translation.** A teacher's output distribution at position *t* is, in current KD, treated as an independent supervisory signal. The full KD loss is `Σ_t KL(T_t || S_t)` — a sum, hence by construction *reducible*. The student is asked to match each marginal, never the joint cause-effect structure across positions, layers, or tokens. In IIT terms, the student inherits the teacher's *extrinsic* outputs but not its *intrinsic* integrated structure — the φ goes to zero in transit. A perfect student under sum-decomposable KD is a philosophical zombie of its teacher: it produces the same surface behaviour and contains none of the structure that produced it.

**Sutra thought experiment.** Define a *bipartition KD loss*: for each minibatch, randomly partition positions into two halves A and B. Compute the teacher's joint distribution over (A,B) and the student's. Then measure the *integration gap*: KL(T_AB || T_A ⊗ T_B) − KL(S_AB || S_A ⊗ S_B). This is the difference in how much extra information the joint carries beyond the marginals — it is φ-like at the distributional level. A student that matches teacher marginals but has zero integration gap is high-φ-loss; a student that matches teacher integration is doing *integrated* distillation. Penalise the integration gap directly. For Sutra-Dyad at 188M bytes, this could be the missing pressure that classical KD never applies.

**What it reframes.** The classical KD plateau is not "the student has saturated"; it is "the student has saturated on the *reducible* subspace and has no gradient toward the *irreducible* subspace." Bits-per-byte measures sum-decomposable performance and is therefore *constitutionally blind* to the gap. The teacher's competence on hard tokens (rare reasoning steps, long-range agreement, structural disambiguation) lives almost entirely in the irreducible part — exactly where standard KD has zero signal.

**Smallest testable prediction.** Train two students from the same init: (A) standard sum-decomposable KD, (B) KD + 0.1× bipartition integration penalty over windows of 8 positions. Prediction: (B) shows ≤0.05 BPB difference vs (A) on average held-out text but ≥0.5 BPB advantage on a held-out subset of *long-range agreement* sentences (subject-verb agreement across 20+ tokens, anaphora resolution across paragraphs). The integration gap reveals itself precisely on the structures that ordinary BPB averages out.

## 2. Global Workspace Theory — KD as Access Policy Transfer

**Mechanism (one sentence).** Global Workspace Theory (Baars, Dehaene) posits that conscious content is whatever wins a competition for *broadcast* into a global workspace where it becomes available to many specialised modules — most cognition is unconscious local processing; consciousness is the *gating* function that selects what gets shared.

**ML translation.** A teacher's intelligence is partly *what it computes* and partly *what it ignores*. Attention patterns, gate values, residual stream norms — these encode a learned access policy: which features get amplified and routed forward, which get suppressed. Standard KD copies the *broadcast content* (the output distribution) but does not copy the *access policy* (the gating that produced it). The student must rediscover, from outputs alone, the entire upstream selection process. This is information-theoretically possible but practically catastrophic — it is the inverse problem with the most-collapsed observation.

**Sutra thought experiment.** Define a *workspace distillation loss* that does not match teacher activations directly but matches the teacher's *competition outcomes*: for each layer, identify the top-k positions by residual stream norm (the "broadcast set") and ask the student to place its top-k broadcast set on the same positions. This is a permutation-/scale-invariant transfer of the access policy without binding the student to the teacher's exact representational geometry. For Sutra-Dyad's byte-level student facing token-level teachers, this matters double: byte-level positions and token-level positions cannot align directly, but *which information dominates the residual stream* is a topological invariant that crosses tokenizers. Workspace transfer is a candidate Ekalavya-compatible cross-architecture channel.

**What it reframes.** "Multi-teacher KD is hard because tokenizers differ" is the wrong diagnosis. The deeper issue: we have been trying to transfer *content under a coordinate system* when the consciousness-science answer is to transfer *the access policy*, which is a coordinate-free object. A workspace is defined by what wins competitions, not by where things sit. Two teachers with different tokenizers can still agree on "this clause is the load-bearing part of this sentence" — that agreement is what should transfer.

**Smallest testable prediction.** Compute teacher residual stream norms per byte-window (averaged over the teacher's tokens that overlap each byte window). Train Sutra-Dyad with a small auxiliary loss matching the rank order of student residual norms to teacher norms within each window. Prediction: the workspace-loss student matches plain-KD BPB within 0.05 but shows materially better long-range coherence in qualitative generation (no topic drift across paragraphs), because the access policy is what enforces topical persistence.

## 3. Higher Order Theories — Distilling the Teacher's Self-Reads

**Mechanism (one sentence).** Higher Order Theories (HOT — Rosenthal, Lau, Brown) hold that a mental state is conscious iff the system has a *higher-order representation of that state* — first-order representation alone is mere processing; consciousness is the system reading its own first-order activity and labeling it as occurrent.

**ML translation.** A trained teacher computes more than next-token distributions. Internally, it represents which of *its own* features are reliable, which are uncertain, which are being ignored. Modern interpretability work (probing classifiers, sparse autoencoder features, induction-head detection) shows that higher-order structure is *latent and recoverable*. Standard KD distills only first-order outputs. A HOT-style KD would distill the teacher's higher-order reads — its meta-features about its own computation — to the student. The student would inherit not only "what to predict" but "how to know when its own prediction is trustworthy."

**Sutra thought experiment.** Train a small probe (a few thousand parameters) on the teacher to predict the teacher's *own future loss* at each position from its current internal activations. This is a higher-order reliability signal — the teacher's metacognitive read of itself. Distill this signal into the student as an auxiliary head: the student must match not only the teacher's output but the teacher's confidence-about-its-output. Now the student has a learned uncertainty head grounded in the teacher's metacognition. For inference efficiency (Outcome 5), this is the load-bearing signal: the student knows when it can exit early because it inherited the teacher's self-knowledge, not just its competence.

**What it reframes.** Calibration in distilled models has been treated as a post-hoc patch (temperature scaling, conformal prediction). HOT reframes it as a *primary distillation channel*: confidence is not a side-effect of the right output distribution; it is a separately learnable representation that the teacher already has and the student does not. Multi-teacher KD especially needs this — the student must learn *which teacher to trust on which inputs*, which is a meta-question only solvable with higher-order representations.

**Smallest testable prediction.** Add a higher-order distillation head to Sutra-Dyad that predicts teacher next-byte loss from student activations. Prediction: ECE (expected calibration error) on held-out text drops by ≥30% vs plain-KD baseline at matched BPB, and an early-exit policy gated on this head's prediction yields ≥1.5× inference speedup at <0.05 BPB cost — neither of which a first-order-only student can achieve without a separate, expensive calibration phase.

## 4. Attention Schema Theory — Distilling the Self-Model of Attention

**Mechanism (one sentence).** Attention Schema Theory (Graziano) proposes that the brain constructs a simplified, schematic model *of its own attention process* — a low-dimensional caricature that the system uses to predict and control where attention will go next; consciousness is the brain modelling itself attending.

**ML translation.** A transformer teacher's attention maps are O(layers × heads × T²) tensors — far too large to distill directly into a 188M student that does not even share the teacher's tokenization. But the *schema* of teacher attention — which broad regions of context typically dominate, what types of dependencies are typically active at this depth — is a tiny, transferable abstraction. Distill the schema, not the attention. The student does not need to reproduce the teacher's exact attention weights; it needs an inductive bias telling it where attention *should* go for inputs of this kind.

**Sutra thought experiment.** Train a 1M-parameter "attention schema network" that, given a window of bytes, predicts a coarse summary of the teacher's attention pattern (e.g., "long-range copy from position [200..220]," "local n-gram pattern," "syntactic head at position 47"). The schema is small, cross-tokenizer-portable (operates on bytes), and reusable across teachers. Distill the schema into Sutra-Dyad as an auxiliary input or as a regulariser on student attention. The student inherits *attention competence* without inheriting attention *geometry*. For a byte-level model facing token-level teachers, this could be the long-sought interface layer — the tokenizer mismatch is dissolved at the schema level.

**What it reframes.** "Attention transfer doesn't work across architectures" is true if you try to transfer the maps; it may be wildly wrong if you transfer the schema. The field has been distilling the wrong object. The schema is also *where the binding problem is solved* in the teacher (next section): it is the unified representation of "what is currently being held together as one thing" — and this is far more transferable than the distributed substrate that implements it.

**Smallest testable prediction.** Train the attention schema network on a frozen teacher (cheap: 1M params, days on CPU). Use it as a soft regulariser on Sutra-Dyad attention. Prediction: on long-context (>4K bytes) coherence tasks, the schema-regularised student outperforms plain-KD by ≥10% on held-out coherence metrics, because the schema injects long-range structure that BPB-only training cannot find at this scale.

## 5. Recurrent Processing Theory — The Missing Recurrent Sweep

**Mechanism (one sentence).** Lamme's Recurrent Processing Theory holds that a fast feedforward sweep through visual cortex suffices for categorisation, but *phenomenal experience and binding* require a subsequent recurrent sweep that re-enters and integrates the early representations — feedforward processing is necessary but insufficient for conscious content.

**ML translation.** A standard transformer is a *deep* feedforward stack — each layer runs once per token, no re-entry. The teacher's "binding" — its ability to hold "the red square to the left of the blue circle" as a single bound percept — is implemented in the depth and width of the feedforward sweep, exhausting whatever binding capacity that architecture allows. A student that matches teacher outputs token-by-token may be *categorising* like the teacher without *binding* like the teacher: the same labels, none of the unifying structure. RPT predicts this mismatch will surface specifically on tasks that require integration across multiple features — exactly the tasks classical KD students fail on (counterfactual reasoning, long-range coreference, compositional generalisation).

**Sutra thought experiment.** Add a learned *recurrent re-entry pass* to Sutra-Dyad: after the feedforward sweep produces logits, feed a low-dimensional summary of the output back as conditioning to a small subset of middle layers and re-run them once. This is cheap (one extra partial sweep) but architecturally introduces the recurrence RPT identifies as binding-essential. Distill the teacher's *post-binding* representation (final layer activations) into the *post-re-entry* student state, not the post-feedforward state. The recurrent sweep gives the student a binding pass it did not architecturally have. This is one of the few mechanisms that could let a small student inherit a large teacher's binding competence without inheriting its parameter count.

**What it reframes.** "Depth is the path to capacity" is the feedforward orthodoxy. RPT says depth gets you categorisation; *recurrence* gets you binding. We have been buying parameter count when we should have been buying re-entry. For Sutra's parameter budget (188M, target eventually 4B), one recurrent sweep through 4 middle layers is far cheaper than 30 extra feedforward layers, and may unlock the specific competences (binding, coreference, compositional integration) that classical KD students lack. Pure-feedforward students of recurrent or test-time-compute teachers (o1, R1, Universal Transformer) are *architecturally incapable* of inheriting the teacher's binding sweep, no matter how good the loss.

**Smallest testable prediction.** Add a single 4-layer recurrent re-entry pass to Sutra-Dyad (≤8M extra params). Prediction: on bound-feature tasks (BLiMP agreement, COGS compositional generalisation, anaphora resolution), the recurrent variant outperforms the feedforward control by ≥15% relative, while on bag-of-features tasks (per-token BPB) the gap is ≤0.05 BPB. The dissociation itself is the evidence — it is exactly what RPT predicts.

## CROSS-POLLINATION

Pairings within this portfolio and with neighbours:

- **IIT × Information Theory (02).** The bipartition integration loss (model 1) is a direct dual to mutual-information-style penalties in portfolio 02. IIT's φ is the limit of a hierarchy of MI-based decompositions (Williams-Beer PID, integrated information decomposition). The two portfolios should converge on a *partial information decomposition* loss for KD: separate unique, redundant, and synergistic teacher information and weight them differently in the student objective. Synergy is what classical KD never transfers.

- **Global Workspace × CRDTs (11).** A workspace policy is exactly the kind of object that should be transferred as a CRDT-shaped constraint set across multiple teachers (portfolio 11, model 4). Each teacher proposes a broadcast set; merge by intersection (or weighted vote). Workspace + CRDTs together give Ekalavya a *content-free, coordinate-free, order-independent* multi-teacher channel.

- **HOT × Predictive Processing.** Higher-order representations and prediction-error signals are dual: a predictive-processing teacher already has an explicit error signal at every layer; that error signal *is* a higher-order read of first-order activations. Distilling the teacher's prediction errors layer-by-layer is a HOT-style transfer with a PP-style implementation. The "free energy" framing from physics portfolio 03 is the same object viewed thermodynamically — we have triangulated.

- **Attention Schema × Linguistics (08) × CS Abstract Interpretation (11).** A schema is a *sound abstract interpreter* of the underlying attention process (portfolio 11, model 1) and is naturally expressed in terms of linguistic constituency / dependency structure (portfolio 08). The schema network is the place where consciousness, CS, and linguistics meet: a small, abstract, sound, structurally-typed model of teacher attention. This is the strongest single cross-pollination in the series — three independent portfolios converging on the same construct.

- **Recurrent Processing × Dynamical Systems (07).** A re-entry sweep is a fixed-point computation; the binding it produces is the attractor state of that recursion. Portfolio 07's mental models (limit cycles, attractor transitions) provide the mathematics; RPT provides the cognitive justification. Together they predict that the *number of re-entry steps needed* should scale with task binding complexity — testable, and a possible source of adaptive-compute signal for Outcome 5.

- **All five × Multi-Source Knowledge Absorption (Outcome 4).** The recurring theme: classical KD transfers *first-order, sum-decomposable, coordinate-bound, feedforward-categorisable* outputs. Every consciousness theory identifies a *different missing dimension* of what real cognition does beyond that. Each missing dimension is a candidate distillation channel that classical KD has not used, and each could be a several-bit-per-byte improvement *that does not require larger teachers*. This is the deepest argument so far for why Ekalavya's plateau is not a teacher-size problem but a *channel-design* problem — and consciousness science is the field that has spent 30 years cataloguing the channels.

The emergent claim: the next decade of KD research is the systematic enumeration of these channels. Consciousness science has done most of the inventory. We just need to import it.
