Neural Tanner-graph decoding is old: [Nachmani et al. 2016](https://doi.org/10.1109/ALLERTON.2016.7852251), syndrome-based training is old: [Lugosch & Gross 2018](https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.10902), and transformerized variable/check interaction is already in the literature: [CrossMPT 2024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.01033) and [MM-ECCT 2025](https://doi.org/10.1109/JSAC.2025.3559154). I did not find a primary-source LM paper using this exact idea as a causal scratchpad, so the narrow novelty claim can be “LDPC-inspired syndrome side-channel inside Sutra,” not “LDPC-style neural syndrome memory is new.”
